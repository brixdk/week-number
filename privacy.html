1. What Information Do We Collect?

Personal Information You Disclose to Us

In Short: We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you connect your Google account to the extension.

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you choose to connect your Google Calendar. The personal information we collect depends on the context of your interactions with us and the Services, the choices you make, and the features you use. The personal information we collect may include the following:

Google User Data We Access:

Calendar Data (via calendar.readonly scope):

  • Calendar event details: titles, descriptions, start times, end times, locations
  • Event participants: attendee email addresses, display names, and response statuses
  • Event metadata: color IDs, HTML links to events, all-day event indicators
  • Video conference links (Google Meet/Hangout links)
  • Calendar settings: timezone, locale preferences, time format (12/24 hour), date format preferences

Profile Data (via userinfo.email scope):

  • Your email address
  • Your name (when available)

Information Automatically Collected

In Short: Some information is collected automatically when you use our Services.

We automatically collect certain information when you use the extension. This information does not reveal your specific identity but may include:

  • User preferences: theme selection (light/dark/system), week start day preference (Sunday/Monday), sync preferences
  • OAuth authentication tokens (stored securely in Chrome's local storage)
  • Cached calendar events (stored temporarily to improve performance)
  • Timestamp of last calendar synchronization

All this information is stored locally on your device and is never transmitted to our servers.

2. How Do We Process Your Information?

In Short: We process your information to provide, improve, and administer our Services, and to comply with legal obligations.

We process your personal information for a variety of reasons, depending on how you interact with our Services, including:

  • To display week numbers: Calculate and display the current week number in the browser extension badge based on your week start preference
  • To show your calendar: Display your calendar events in a monthly view within the extension popup
  • To match your preferences: Synchronize with your Google Calendar settings (timezone, time format) for consistent display
  • To provide event details: Show comprehensive event information including time, location, attendees, and video conference links
  • To improve performance: Cache calendar data locally to reduce repeated API requests and provide faster loading times

Important: All data processing occurs locally within your Chrome browser. We do NOT:

  • Send your data to any third-party servers
  • Share your data with any external services
  • Use your data for advertising or analytics
  • Sell or monetize your personal information

3. What Legal Bases Do We Rely On to Process Your Information?

In Short: We only process your personal information when we believe it is necessary and we have a valid legal reason to do so.

We process your personal information based on:

  • Consent: You provide explicit consent when you connect your Google Calendar to the extension through OAuth 2.0 authorization
  • Legitimate Interests: We process your information to provide and improve the extension functionality you requested

You may withdraw your consent at any time by disconnecting your Google Calendar through the extension settings or by uninstalling the extension.

4. When and With Whom Do We Share Your Personal Information?

In Short: We do not share your information with third parties.

We do NOT share, sell, rent, or trade your personal information with third parties for their promotional purposes.

The only data transmission that occurs is between:

  • Your browser and Google's Calendar API: To fetch your calendar data directly from Google's servers
  • Your browser and Google's OAuth servers: For secure authentication purposes

No intermediary servers are involved. Your data flows directly from Google to your browser and stays on your device.

5. How Long Do We Keep Your Information?

In Short: We keep your information only as long as necessary for the purposes set out in this privacy notice.

We store your information locally on your device:

  • OAuth tokens: Stored securely using Chrome Identity API until you disconnect or revoke access
  • Calendar events: Cached temporarily and automatically refreshed every 30 minutes
  • User preferences: Stored until you change them, disconnect the extension, or uninstall it

When you disconnect your Google Calendar or uninstall the extension, all stored data is automatically deleted from your device.

6. How Do We Keep Your Information Safe?

In Short: We aim to protect your personal information through a system of organizational and technical security measures.

We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect the security of any personal information we process. These measures include:

  • Using Google's official OAuth 2.0 authentication flow (industry standard for secure authorization)
  • Never handling or storing your Google password
  • Storing authentication tokens using Chrome's secure storage APIs
  • Requesting only read-only access to your calendar (we cannot modify or delete your events)
  • Processing all data locally within your browser's secure, sandboxed environment
  • No server-side storage or processing of your personal data

However, please note that no electronic transmission over the internet or information storage technology can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. While we implement reasonable security measures, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. What Are Your Privacy Rights?

In Short: You have complete control over your data and can access, change, or delete your information at any time.

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Access: View all stored data through Chrome's developer tools (chrome://extensions)
  • Right to Revoke: Disconnect your Google Calendar at any time through the extension settings
  • Right to Delete: All data is automatically cleared when you disconnect or uninstall the extension
  • Right to Control Sync: Enable or disable calendar synchronization through extension settings
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Revoke OAuth access through Google Account Permissions

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights:

  1. Open the Week Number extension
  2. Click the settings icon
  3. Use the "Disconnect" option to revoke access and clear all data

Alternatively, you can:

  • Visit your Google Account Permissions page and remove Week Number's access
  • Uninstall the extension from chrome://extensions

9. Third-Party Services

The extension integrates with the following Google services:

  • Google Calendar API: For accessing your calendar data (subject to Google APIs Terms of Service)
  • Google OAuth 2.0: For secure authentication (managed entirely by Google)

These are the only third-party services used by Week Number. We do not integrate with any analytics, advertising, or tracking services.
